Cancel culture

[last update: 1 Dec 2020]

Es una amenaza al FS pero está basada en el ejercicio legítimo del FS → ¡aporía! Cosas →

Ben Porath

“…people are removed from prominent positions on account of an ideological breach; they say something that goes against what others see as permissible. When we talk about canceling a person, I think what we’re talking about is the act of removing them from a position of prominence because of their views, a statement that they make, or ideas that they stand for.”

¿Qué pasa con cosas viejas que se publicaron pre arribo a posición / status de public figure?

Reacciones a la carta abierta de Harper’s.

Moyn:

“I am not a free speech absolutist. Language is part of how our world is constituted. It does not operate free from the dangers and hierarchies of real life; it makes them possible. Calls for open debate routinely conceal the endurance of hierarchies. Distinguishing between necessarily helpful speech and potentially harmful acts, as John Stuart Mill did and as free speech absolutists do, will not work. And without necessarily incurring the risk of slippery slopes, we can ban—or even empower the state to do so. We can cancel too.”

Vox → “For weeks now, the American intellectual elite has seemed to be engaged in a bitter war over free speech. I say “seems” because the conflict is not really about free speech — at least not when you take a closer look”.
